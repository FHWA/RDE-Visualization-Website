RDE DATA VISUALIZATION

Impact of Communication

The Connected Vehicle Safety Pilot Program is a research initiative that features real-world implementation of connected vehicle safety technologies, applications, and systems using everyday drivers. The Safety Pilot Model Deployment (SPMD), conducted in Ann Arbor, Michigan, was a data collection effort under the program.

Most of the data collected during the pilot was obtained from on-board vehicle devices and roadside equipment (RSE). The vehicle devices broadcast Basic Safety Messages (BSM) at a rate of 10Hz. During the deployment, RSEs were placed around Ann Arbor and constantly received messages within an estimated range of 300 meters.

The vehicles were equipped to constantly send data via Vehicle 2 Vehicle (V2V) and Vehicle 2 Infrastructure (V2I) messaging. These messages were sent out at 10 Hz constantly as a sort of "heartbeat message". These heartbeat messages were to alert other vehicles and infrastructure to the cars' position/heading/speed/acceleration. In addition to the basic heartbeat messages, the vehicles could also send optional data if an 'event' occurs. This includes path history, path prediction, and event flags. All of these messages were collected by the vehicles by a WSU (wireless safety unit) and logged in the BSMp1 file. The RSE receives both the heartbeat and optional data with its own sensors and stores the data. The data stored by the vehicles and RSE are slightly different due to recording instruments they use.

Data Inconsistency

Due to the differences in data recording mentioned above, there are some instances where the latitude and longitude of the messages sent and received are not the same. This means there are some instances where it appears that more messages are received than actually sent in an area. In the following visualization elements, these instances are colored red.


Another source of inconsistency is errors in the GPS unit of the vehicles. There are a few messages that appear to be sent from inside the river or other locations not near a road. These messages are most likely errors in the GPS reading for latitude and longitude.

BSM Receive Rate

The roadside unit #153 was located at the intersection of N Main St and Depot St. The next closest RSE was one kilometer away, so RSE #153 is good for looking at all the messages sent and received within 500 meters of its location. The BSM logs from April 2013 show over 13 million messages were sent by equipped vehicles, and almost 2 million were received by RSE #153.
