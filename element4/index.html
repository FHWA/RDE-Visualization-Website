RDE DATA VISUALIZATION

Impact of Interventions

Multi-Modal Intelligent Traffic Signal Systems (MMITSS) is a technology designed to allow traffic signals to communicate with vehicles and possibly behave differently for different types of vehicles. For example, emergency vehicles, transit vehicles (such as buses), or trucks may need to request specific signal phases as they approach intersections.

Multiple aspects of MMITSS were field-tested in Anthem, Arizona, including two signal priority scenarios.

Freight Signal Priority

In the Freight Signal Priority (FSP) scenario, trucks were allowed to request signal changes at an intersection. Delay at the intersection is visualized below; darker bars mean more delay in a time interval.

Transit Signal Priority/Freight Signal Priority

In the Transit Signal Priority (TSP)/Freight Signal Priority (FSP) Bundle scenario, both buses and trucks were allowed to request signal changes at an intersection; however, the test buses were all traveling East/West, and the test trucks were all traveling North/South. Methodology

These visualizations use the MMITSS data on the Research Data Exchange. We used the GPS and BSM data to get positions of all instrumented vehicles, filtered out all positions greater than 50 meters from the center of the Daisy Mountain Dr/Gavilan Peak Pkwy intersection, and additionally filtered out all positions where the vehicle's speed was greater than 5 km/h. After downsampling from 10Hz to 1Hz, we counted the number of points in each time interval to yield the total vehicle-seconds of delay in the time interval.

For a more formal assessment of the impact of MMITSS, see the MMITSS Impact Assessment.
